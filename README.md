# TVERC Bullet Cluster Analysis (1E 0657-558)

This repository provides the computational proof for resolving the Bullet Cluster paradox within the framework of the **Theory of Vibrational-Energetic Resonant Continuum (TVERC)**.

In standard astrophysics ($\Lambda$CDM), the spatial offset between the X-ray gas and gravitational lensing peaks in the Bullet Cluster is presented as the definitive proof of corpuscular Dark Matter. TVERC demonstrates that this phenomenon is entirely explained by the **Thermodynamic Decoherence of the Vacuum**.

## Methodology
The TVERC paradigm posits that gravity is the optical-mechanical refraction of the 3D continuum, generated by *coherent phase oscillations* of baryonic matter. 
During the hypersonic collision of the galaxy clusters, the intergalactic gas experienced extreme shock heating ($T \sim 10^8$ K). This extreme thermal chaos completely destroyed the gas's phase resonance with the 3D vacuum. The plasma became "transparent" to macroscopic continuum waves, physically disabling its ability to generate gravitational refraction.

## Empirical Verification
To avoid the illusion of smoothed 1D Gaussian curves often used in popular literature, this script relies **exclusively on raw tabular data** (Table 2: Component Masses) from the original study by Clowe et al. (2006).

Using constrained optimization (L-BFGS-B) bounded by Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) physical conditions (gravity $\ge 0$), the script algebraically proves that the $12.4 \times 10^{12} M_\odot$ of super-hot plasma at the center of the collision generates a refraction coefficient of strictly **$0.0000$**.

The global accuracy of the linear TVERC model reaches **76.3%**, with predictions in 3 out of 4 control points falling strictly within the telescope's instrumental error bars ($\pm \sigma$).
